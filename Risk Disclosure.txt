PLEASE READ THIS RISK DISCLOSURE CAREFULLY. IT DESCRIBES MATERIAL RISKS

BY USING THE PLATFORM, YOU ACKNOWLEDGE THAT YOU HAVE READ AND UNDERSTOOD THESE
RISKS AND ACCEPT THEM IN FULL.

================================================================================

1. YOU CAN LOSE EVERYTHING

Participation in token launches, purchases, or any activity on Mammoth Protocol
may result in the total and permanent loss of all funds involved. Cryptocurrency
and token markets are highly volatile. There is no guarantee that any token will
retain any value. Do not invest more than you can afford to lose entirely.

================================================================================

2. SMART CONTRACT RISK

Mammoth Protocol operates through smart contracts deployed on the Solana
blockchain. Smart contracts may contain bugs, errors, or vulnerabilities that
are not apparent even after code review or audit. Such vulnerabilities could be
exploited by malicious actors, resulting in partial or total loss of funds held
in or interacting with those contracts.

The Company does not guarantee that its smart contracts are free from
vulnerabilities. No smart contract audit constitutes a guarantee of security.
Past security does not guarantee future security.

================================================================================

3. BLOCKCHAIN NETWORK RISK

The Solana blockchain, like all blockchain networks, may experience:
(a) Network outages, downtime, or degraded performance
(b) Congestion that delays or prevents transaction confirmation
(c) Protocol upgrades, hard forks, or consensus failures
(d) Validator failures or collusion

Any of these events could affect your ability to transact, exercise rights, or
access your tokens. The Company has no control over the Solana network.

================================================================================

4. TRANSACTION IRREVERSIBILITY

All transactions on the Solana blockchain are irreversible once confirmed.
This includes token purchases, rights exercises, cycle activations, and treasury
distributions. There is no mechanism to cancel, reverse, or recover a confirmed
transaction. Verify all transaction parameters carefully before signing.

================================================================================

5. SCHEDULED LAUNCH AND TIME-LOCK RISK

When a creator deploys a token with a scheduled launch time, that time is
encoded immutably in the smart contract. The launch will occur at the specified
time regardless of market conditions, creator intent, or any other factor.
Neither the creator nor the Company can delay, cancel, or modify a scheduled
launch once the transaction is signed. Creators should carefully consider timing
before committing to a scheduled launch.

================================================================================

6. RIGHTS WINDOW RISK

Rights-based anti-dilution windows are time-limited. If you fail to exercise
your rights within the designated window, those rights will expire permanently.
You will not be able to exercise expired rights, and the Company has no ability
to restore them. Monitor active cycles and rights windows carefully.

================================================================================

7. TOKEN VALUE RISK

Tokens created on Mammoth Protocol:
(a) Have no intrinsic value guaranteed by any person, entity, or asset.
(b) Are not backed by any government, central bank, or reserve.
(c) May have no utility beyond the Platform or ecosystem in which they are used.
(d) May trade at any price determined by market participants, including zero.
(e) May be subject to extreme price volatility at any time.

Bonding curve price projections displayed on the Platform are mathematical
models, not guarantees or predictions of future price.

================================================================================

8. LIQUIDITY RISK

Tokens created on Mammoth Protocol may have limited or no secondary market
liquidity. You may be unable to sell your tokens at any price, or at all,
particularly between cycles or for newly launched tokens with limited adoption.
The existence of a Jupiter deeplink does not guarantee liquidity or a market.

================================================================================

9. REGULATORY RISK

The regulatory environment for cryptocurrency and digital tokens is rapidly
evolving and uncertain. Regulatory action by governments or agencies including
but not limited to the SEC, CFTC, FinCEN, or equivalent international bodies
could:
(a) Classify tokens as securities, commodities, or other regulated instruments.
(b) Restrict or prohibit your ability to hold, trade, or use tokens.
(c) Result in enforcement actions, fines, or asset freezes.
(d) Require the Platform to cease operations in certain jurisdictions.

The Company makes no representation as to the regulatory status of any token
on the Platform. You are responsible for your own regulatory compliance.

================================================================================

10. CREATOR RISK

Tokens on Mammoth Protocol are created by independent third parties, not by the
Company. The Company does not:
(a) Vet, endorse, or verify token creators or their projects.
(b) Guarantee that creators will open future cycles, continue development, or
    fulfill any stated goals or roadmap.
(c) Have any recourse against creators who abandon projects or act in bad faith.

Purchasing a token does not establish any contractual relationship between you
and the creator beyond what is encoded in the smart contract. Cycle parameters
are set by creators and are immutable once deployed. Creators may terminate
cycles early, choose not to open future cycles, or set hard caps at any time.

================================================================================

11. TREASURY DISTRIBUTION RISK

Treasury routing parameters are set by creators at cycle creation and are
immutable. The Company does not control how creators configure treasury splits.
There is no escrow, lock-up, or vesting requirement on creator treasury
distributions. A creator may receive their treasury allocation immediately upon
cycle close.

================================================================================

12. PROTOCOL FEE RISK

Mammoth Protocol charges a 2% fee on trades routed through the Mammoth
interface. This fee is non-refundable and is deducted automatically. The Company
reserves the right to modify fee structures, which could affect the economics
of token purchases.

================================================================================

13. THIRD-PARTY INTEGRATION RISK

The Platform integrates with third-party services including Jupiter Protocol,
Solana RPC providers, and wallet applications. Failures, vulnerabilities, or
changes in these third-party services could affect Platform functionality.
The Company is not responsible for losses arising from third-party service
failures.

================================================================================

14. CYBERSECURITY RISK

The Platform and its infrastructure may be targeted by hackers, phishing
attacks, or other malicious actors. While the Company implements security
measures, no system is completely secure. You are responsible for securing
your own wallet credentials and private keys. The Company will never ask for
your seed phrase or private keys.

================================================================================

15. NO INSURANCE

Cryptocurrency assets, including tokens on Mammoth Protocol, are not covered
by any government deposit insurance scheme (such as FDIC or SIPC in the United
States) or any private insurance arrangement provided by the Company.

================================================================================

16. EXPERIMENTAL TECHNOLOGY

Mammoth Protocol is built on experimental technology. The Solana blockchain,
SPL token standard, and the Mammoth smart contracts are all relatively new and
may behave in unexpected ways. Features may change, fail, or be deprecated.
The Platform is currently operating on Solana Devnet (test network) and will
transition to Mainnet. Behavior on Mainnet may differ from Devnet.

================================================================================

Cryptocurrency transactions may be taxable events in your jurisdiction. Buying,
selling, or receiving tokens may trigger capital gains, income tax, or other tax
obligations. The Company does not provide tax advice. You are solely responsible
for determining and meeting your own tax obligations.
